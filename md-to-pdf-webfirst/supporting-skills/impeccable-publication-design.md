# Impeccable Publication Design Adapter

Use this local adapter for font, color, and publication-readability decisions in research-report PDFs.

## Typography

- Chinese report titles must carry real weight: use a strong Song/serif display face when available, with a heavy sans fallback.
- Body text must stay readable in print. Do not compress body or table text to meet an arbitrary page count.
- Use at most three font roles: brand/display, body, and numeric/mono labels.
- Use generous line height for Chinese paragraphs; keep paragraphs short and editorial.
- English brand marks or running headers may use condensed uppercase styling, but never at the cost of readability.

## Color

- Build a restrained publication palette with clear roles: ink/navy for authority, red for diagnosis/alert, teal/green for resilience, neutral paper for surfaces.
- Avoid one-note beige pages, generic purple-blue gradients, and large decorative blobs.
- Use red sparingly and consistently; red should mean risk, section marker, or diagnosis emphasis.
- Tables and charts need enough contrast in print grayscale: do not rely only on subtle tints.

## Layout Discipline

- Start important pages with one clear assertion, then show the visual or data proof.
- Use stable running elements: report name, institution/brand, section label, page number, figure/table number, and source note.
- Do not use side-stripe callout cards as a default report texture. Use real hierarchy, tables, figures, and editorial spacing instead.
- Every figure page should look intentional on its own: title, figure number, visual, source, and footnote when needed.
